On **2026-06-26**, two related things happened at the US frontier-AI frontier. **OpenAI began a limited preview of GPT-5.6 Sol with the customer list "shared with the government"** ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/)). On the same day, **the Commerce Department lifted its two-week-old export block on Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5**, releasing it to *"more than 100 US institutions, including major companies and government agencies"* via a letter from Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick ([Semafor](https://www.semafor.com/article/06/27/2026/us-releases-powerful-anthropic-model-mythos-to-some-us-companies)). Together the two actions establish a new distribution model: each new customer of a US frontier lab's most capable model is, in practice, approved by the Trump administration's Commerce Department — with no Federal Register entry, no executive order, and no published criteria. Semafor: *"the framework for overseeing AI is being built on the fly."* OpenAI is publicly pushing back: *"We don't believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default."* **The policy is the story. The model releases are evidence.**

## What happened — the regime

OpenAI's Sol preview is *"a limited preview for a small group of trusted partners whose participation has been shared with the government."* The Lutnick letter grants a license exemption for *"entities identified in Annex A to this letter and their foreign national employees, or to Anthropic's foreign national employees."* The [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/06/26/openai-says-us-government-will-vet-users-its-latest-ai-model/) reports the government reserves the right to *"change the list of companies at any time."* Annex A is not public.

**The structural shift: a new customer-vetting regime, applied to both labs.** Per WaPo: *"The Trump administration is requiring both Anthropic and OpenAI to get approval for each new customer of their most powerful AI technology."* WaPo: *"The Trump administration is **expanding** its recent policy"* — the OpenAI intervention *"was the first time it had extended government vetting of AI customers beyond Anthropic."*

**No published rule.** Semafor: *"the framework for overseeing AI is being built on the fly, and many users of the powerful tools — from non-US governments and companies to consumers — remain in the dark."* OpenAI names a *"cyber Executive Order framework"* as the path out, but no such order has been published as of 2026-06-27. Commerce spokesman Benno Kass: *"In just two weeks, we have worked diligently to ensure America remains the global leader in AI while safeguarding our security."*

**OpenAI pushes back.** *"We don't believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default. It keeps the best tools from users, developers, enterprises, cyber defenders, and global partners who need them."* Sam Altman on X: *"I just dont like the idea of the government picking the customers … Confident we will get to a better place."*

**Allied reaction.** Semafor: *"European officials and other US allies have expressed frustration at their new dependence on decisions in Washington."* Britain's AI Security Institute is the only non-U.S. entity with access to GPT-5.6 (WaPo).

## What happened — the model evidence

**GPT-5.6 series: Sol, Terra, Luna.** Pricing per 1M tokens: Sol **$5 / $30**; Terra **$2.50 / $15**; Luna **$1 / $6**. Caching: 1.25x write, 90% read discount, 30-minute minimum cache life, explicit cache breakpoints. New modes: a `max` reasoning effort and an `ultra` mode that *"goes beyond the capabilities of a single agent by leveraging subagents."*

**Capability claims — all vendor-attributed.** Terminal-Bench 2.1 SOTA; ExploitBench² *"competitive with Mythos Preview using only ~1/3 of the output tokens"*; ExploitGym (UC Berkeley + OpenAI + other labs) *"strong improvements in cyber capabilities."* Per OpenAI: *"GPT-5.6 Sol does not cross the Cyber Critical threshold under our Preparedness Framework … it identified bugs and exploitation primitives — but did not autonomously produce a functional full-chain exploit under the conditions tested."* OpenAI's own threshold, OpenAI's own assessment.

**The Mythos 5 letter.** Lutnick to Anthropic's Tom Brown: *"I have determined that appropriate safeguards are in place to permit certain trusted partners to access the Claude Mythos 5 Model."* Semafor: *"The letter is silent on Fable 5 … moving toward releasing Fable as well, though that timeline is unclear."*

## Why it matters

**A structural shift, not an event.** Two frontier labs are now under the same Commerce Department customer-vetting regime, with the same day as the visible evidence. The story is the regime, not the releases. **No published rule** — *"Built on the fly"* is Semafor's direct framing. Rep. Sam Liccardo (D-California) to WaPo: *"The most kleptocratic regime in U.S. history has positioned itself as the unreviewable gatekeeper of the world's most powerful technology, and nobody inside or outside the administration can say what standards it's using."* **OpenAI is publicly unhappy** — the most useful single line in the story for a builder audience. **Allies are now dependent on US government decisions** — the British AI Security Institute as the only non-U.S. entity with access to GPT-5.6 is the documented signal.

## Practical implications

- **Frontier-model procurement is now a regulatory event.** *"no process for individual users to get access to the new model"* (WaPo).
- **GPT-5.6 Terra and Luna are NOT under the customer-vetting regime** — only the Sol preview is.
- **Cache economics change for GPT-5.6:** 30-min minimum cache life, explicit breakpoints, 1.25x write / 90% read.
- **Do not plan production workloads on the Sol preview.** Broader availability is *"in the coming weeks"* — not a date.
- **For non-US and non-Annex-A companies, the Mythos 5 question is the regulatory event of the week.** Per WaPo, the Commerce letter said *"only U.S.-based companies have access."*
