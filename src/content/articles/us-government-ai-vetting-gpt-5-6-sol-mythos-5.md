On **2026-06-26**, two related things happened at the US frontier-AI frontier. **OpenAI began a limited preview of GPT-5.6 Sol with the customer list "shared with the government"** ([OpenAI, 2026-06-26](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/)). On the same day, **the Commerce Department lifted its two-week export block on Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5**, releasing it to *"more than 100 US institutions, including major companies and government agencies"* via a letter from Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick ([Semafor, 2026-06-26, 6:30pm EDT](https://www.semafor.com/article/06/27/2026/us-releases-powerful-anthropic-model-mythos-to-some-us-companies)). Together the two actions establish a new distribution model: each new customer of a US frontier lab's most capable model is, in practice, approved by the Trump administration's Commerce Department. Semafor frames this as *"the framework for overseeing AI is being built on the fly"*; the [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2026/06/26/openai-says-us-government-will-vet-users-its-latest-ai-model/) calls it *"expanding its recent policy of vetting companies that want access to the latest artificial intelligence technology."* No Federal Register entry, no executive order, no published criteria. **The policy is the story. The model releases are evidence.**

## What happened — the regime

OpenAI's announcement describes the GPT-5.6 Sol preview as *"a limited preview for a small group of trusted partners whose participation has been shared with the government."* The Lutnick letter grants a license exemption for *"entities identified in Annex A to this letter and their foreign national employees."* The Annex A list is not public. Per WaPo, *"The Trump administration is requiring both Anthropic and OpenAI to get approval for each new customer of their most powerful AI technology."* The word *"both"* is doing work: this is no longer a one-off Mythos case but a pre-approval regime across the two US frontier labs.

**No public rules.** Semafor: *"The framework for overseeing AI is being built on the fly, and many users of the powerful tools … remain in the dark."* OpenAI names a *"cyber Executive Order framework"* as the path out, but no such order has been published as of 2026-06-27.

**OpenAI pushes back.** *"We don't believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default. It keeps the best tools from users, developers, enterprises, cyber defenders, and global partners who need them. We are taking this short-term step because we believe it is the strongest path to broader availability in the coming weeks."* Sam Altman on X: *"I just don't like the idea of the government picking the customers."* Per Semafor, *"European officials and other US allies have expressed frustration."* Britain's AI Security Institute is the only non-U.S. entity with access to GPT-5.6.

## What happened — the model releases

**GPT-5.6 series, new naming scheme.** Per OpenAI: *"Sol, our flagship model; Terra, a balanced model for everyday work; and Luna, a fast and affordable model. Terra has competitive performance to GPT-5.5 while being 2x cheaper and Luna brings strong capability at our lowest cost."* The number identifies generation; Sol, Terra, Luna identify durable capability tiers.

**Pricing per 1M tokens.** Sol: **$5 input / $30 output**. Terra: **$2.50 / $15**. Luna: **$1 / $6**. On caching: *"cache writes are billed at 1.25x the model's uncached input rate, while cache reads continue to receive the 90% cached-input discount."* A new `max` reasoning effort and an `ultra` mode leveraging subagents ship with Sol.

**Capability claims — all vendor-attributed.** Terminal-Bench 2.1 SOTA. GeneBench v1 *"achieves stronger results than GPT-5.5 while using fewer tokens."* ExploitBench²: *"GPT-5.6 Sol is competitive with Mythos Preview using only ~1/3 of the output tokens."* On the Cyber Critical threshold: *"GPT-5.6 Sol does not cross the Cyber Critical threshold under our Preparedness Framework … it did not autonomously produce a functional full-chain exploit under the conditions tested."* All are OpenAI's own measurements.

**The Mythos 5 letter.** Lutnick to Anthropic: *"I have determined that appropriate safeguards are in place to permit certain trusted partners to access the Claude Mythos 5 Model."* Semafor: *"The letter is silent on Fable 5 … People close to the talks said they are moving toward releasing Fable as well, though that timeline is unclear."*

## Why it matters now

1. **It is a structural shift, not an event.** Two frontier labs are now under the same Commerce Department customer-vetting regime, with the same day as the visible evidence.
2. **The regime is being built without public rules.** *"Built on the fly"* is Semafor's framing. Rep. Sam Liccardo to WaPo: *"the unreviewable gatekeeper of the world's most powerful technology, and nobody inside or outside the administration can say what standards it's using."*
3. **OpenAI is publicly unhappy, in its own announcement.** The most useful single line for a builder audience.
4. **Foreign users, including US allies, are now dependent on US government decisions.** The British AI Security Institute is the only non-U.S. entity with access to GPT-5.6.

## Practical implications

- **Frontier-model procurement is now a regulatory event, not a sales event.** Until the cyber Executive Order lands, the US government is the de facto customer-vetting step for OpenAI Sol and Anthropic Mythos 5.
- **GPT-5.6 Terra and Luna are NOT under the customer-vetting regime**, per OpenAI — the vetting is for the Sol preview.
- **Cache economics change for GPT-5.6.** Cache writes at 1.25x uncached input; 30-minute minimum cache life; explicit cache breakpoints.
- **Do not plan production workloads on the GPT-5.6 Sol preview.** Broader availability is *"in the coming weeks,"* not a calendar date.
- **For non-US and non-Annex-A companies, the Mythos 5 question is the regulatory event of the week.** Per WaPo, *"only U.S.-based companies have access."*

## Risks and caveats

1. **All GPT-5.6 capability claims are OpenAI's.** The article attributes; it does not assert.
2. **"Does not cross the Cyber Critical threshold" is OpenAI's self-assessment under OpenAI's own Preparedness Framework.**
3. **The "new regulatory regime" is Semafor's framing, confirmed by WaPo and OpenAI.** No more specific legal characterisation.
4. **No published rule, no executive order, no Federal Register entry.** The article does not infer a process not in one of the three primary sources.
5. **The Annex A customer list for Mythos 5 is not public.** The article does not name specific customers.
6. **The Fable 5 status is unresolved.** Semafor: *"moving toward releasing Fable as well, though that timeline is unclear."*

## What to watch

1. **The cyber Executive Order itself** — OpenAI names it as the path out.
2. **Fable 5 timeline** (Semafor: *"moving toward releasing Fable as well, though that timeline is unclear"*).
3. **Broad GPT-5.6 availability** (Sol on Cerebras *"at up to 750 tokens per second in July"* is a separate track).
4. **Whether xAI, Google, or Meta are brought into the regime.**
5. **Independent benchmarks of GPT-5.6 Sol.**
6. **The "framework being built on the fly" materialising into a published rule.**
