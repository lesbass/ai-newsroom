## Frontend Design Visual Review (Run 3, 2026-07-20 08:40 UTC)

Per the frontend-design skill: ground review in the subject (evidence-based AI journalism for a technical audience), assess typography/palette/layout/signature, critique against current defaults, and propose concrete improvements.

### Typography & Readability **PASS**
- **Display**: Fraunces (600-700 weight) — a characterful editorial serif with optical sizing. Not a default choice (avoids Inter, Georgia, or system serif).
- **Body**: Atkinson Hyperlegible — designed for maximum legibility by the Braille Institute. Distinctive at reading sizes, high character differentiation.
- **Mono**: IBM Plex Mono (400-700) — clean for code blocks, tags, nav, and UI chrome.
- **Scale**: Article `h1` at `clamp(2.2rem, 6vw, 4.45rem)` with tight 1.02 line-height creates dramatic article headers. Body at 1.0625rem / 1.75 line-height is comfortable on mobile.
- **Font loading**: All font files are self-hosted WOFF2, sub-50KB each, with `font-display: swap`.
- **Critique**: The Fraunces + Atkinson + IBM Plex Mono trio is cohesive and editorial. No changes recommended.

### Color System **PASS**
- **Light**: `#f3f6fb` background (icy blue-white, not the warm-cream `#F4F1EA` default), `#101827` text (near-black with blue undertone), `#1d4f91` accent (deep blue).
- **Dark**: `#0b1020` background (navy, not pure black), `#eef4ff` text, `#7db7ff` accent.
- **Highlight**: `#c2563f` (terra cotta red) used only for high-risk-claim tags and link underlines. Sparing, effective.
- **Critique**: The palette deliberately avoids the three current AI-design defaults (cream+terracotta, black+acid-green, newsprint-hairline). It's cool, calm, and editorial. The highlight red is used with enough restraint that it signals importance without screaming.

### Layout & Structure **PASS**
- **Signature element**: The left-side vertical tick-mark timeline on the hero + the same tick markers on article cards creates a consistent editorial identity. This is the site's "one memorable thing" — it encodes the news-desk signal metaphor into the visual structure.
- **Hero**: Large Fraunces headline (`clamp(3rem, 7vw, 5.8rem)`), max 11ch width, left-aligned — not centered, not templated.
- **Grid background**: 4rem dot-grid with `var(--color-grid)` — adds depth without competing for attention. Smartly disappears behind content.
- **Content width**: 760px max, producing ~65-75 character lines. Good for readability.
- **Card design**: Featured card uses a thick left accent border (`0.45rem`) instead of a box shadow or border-radius-heavy card — consistent with the tick-mark motif.

### Listing / Card Density **PASS**
- **Homepage descriptions**: All are 1-2 concise sentences, no multi-paragraph walls.
- **RSS descriptions**: Similarly compact, 1-2 sentences per item.
- **Tag pages**: Grid layout with count badges, alphabetical ordering, scannable.
- **Article meta line**: Monospace compact row with date, estimated read time, source count — efficient use of horizontal space.

## Fixes Applied Run 3

### 1. Title truncation bug in `<title>` tag ✅ **FIXED**

**Bug**: The `truncateTitle` function in `src/layouts/BaseLayout.astro:22` included `-` (hyphen) in its break-character list. For long article titles containing hyphenated compound words (e.g., "open-weights", "open-source", "Apache-2.0"), the function would break mid-word at the hyphen.

**Example (before fix)**:
- `<title>Moonshot AI releases Kimi K3 — a 2.8T open</title>` (42 chars, broken at hyphen in "open-weights")

**After fix**: Removed `-` from break characters. Titles now break at word boundaries:
- `<title>Moonshot AI releases Kimi K3 — a 2.8T open-weights MoE model with…</title>` (71 chars, clean word break)

**Impact**: 17 article pages had broken `<title>` tags with mid-word hyphen breaks. All now truncate cleanly at word boundaries with ellipsis. OG and Twitter titles were unaffected (they use the full title).

**Verification**: Build succeeds (647 pages, 2.51s). All 5 check scripts pass. Spot-checked all 17 truncated titles — all clean word-boundary breaks.
